About Katy

Katy is a city in southeast Texas located about 30 miles west of Houston. Incorporated in 1945, it sits at the intersection of Harris, Fort Bend, and Waller counties. As of the 2020 U.S. Census, the city had a population of 21,894. Katy has a humid subtropical climate, with hot, humid summers and mild winters. The city’s economy benefits from its proximity to Houston’s energy corridor, and residents enjoy a cost of living that is generally lower than the national average. Katy is served by the highly rated Katy Independent School District and has become a popular suburb for families and professionals seeking access to both quality education and metropolitan amenities.
