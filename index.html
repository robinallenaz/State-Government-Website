About Texas

Did you know? Texas is the only state to have been an independent nation before joining the United States.

Texas is the second largest state in the United States by both area and population. Located in the South Central region, it shares borders with Louisiana, Arkansas, Oklahoma, New Mexico, and the Mexican states of Chihuahua, Coahuila, Nuevo León, and Tamaulipas. Texas is known for its diverse landscapes, ranging from deserts and prairies to forests and coastlines. The state’s economy is one of the largest in the world, with major industries including energy, agriculture, technology, and aerospace. Texas became the 28th state of the U.S. in 1845, and its capital is Austin.

Quick Facts about Texas

  • Texas is the second largest state in the U.S. with 268,820 square miles.
  • Nickname: The Lone Star State, after its flag adopted in 1836.
  • Population: Over 29 million (2020 Census).
  • The Alamo in San Antonio is a symbol of Texas independence.
  • The official small mammal of Texas is the nine-banded armadillo.

The state flower of Texas is the bluebonnet.

Texas was an independent country from 1836 to 1845.
